2) What the DMCA covers

The DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) is a U.S. law that provides a process for copyright owners to request removal of material that allegedly infringes their copyright.

Civic Threat may display:

  • Hosted content (files or text stored on our servers), and
  • Third-party embeds/links (content hosted by other platforms and embedded or linked on our pages).

For third-party embeds/links, the source platform typically controls the content. We may still remove the embed/link from our site if appropriate, but the underlying post must be addressed with the platform that hosts it.

3) How to submit a DMCA Notice (copyright complaint)

To submit a valid DMCA Notice, email ogdeig@protonmail.com with the subject line DMCA Notice and include all of the following:

  • Your full legal name and contact information (email + mailing address).
  • Identification of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed (or a representative list).
  • Specific URL(s) on Civic Threat where the allegedly infringing material appears.
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • A statement that the information in your notice is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the owner.
  • Electronic signature (typing your full legal name is acceptable for email).

Optional but helpful

  • A screenshot highlighting the material
  • Registration number(s) or proof of ownership
  • Explain whether the complaint concerns a hosted file vs. a third-party embed/link

4) What happens after we receive a valid Notice

After we receive a complete and valid DMCA Notice, we may:

  • Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material (where applicable),
  • Notify the person who submitted the content (if we have contact info), and
  • Take reasonable steps to prevent repeat infringement where appropriate.

We may request additional information if your notice is incomplete. We may also decline to act on notices that appear abusive, clearly invalid, or unrelated to copyright.

5) Counter-Notice (if you believe content was removed by mistake)

If you believe your content was removed due to a mistake or misidentification, you may send a counter-notice to ogdeig@protonmail.com with the subject line DMCA Counter-Notice.

A valid counter-notice typically includes:

  • Your full legal name, address, and phone number.
  • The URL or description of the material that was removed and where it appeared before removal.
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  • A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or if outside the U.S., an appropriate U.S. jurisdiction), and that you will accept service of process from the complaining party.
  • Your electronic signature (typing your full legal name is acceptable for email).

6) Repeat infringers

In appropriate circumstances, Civic Threat may restrict or terminate access for users who repeatedly submit infringing material.

7) Misrepresentations

Submitting a false or misleading DMCA notice or counter-notice may have legal consequences. Use this process only for legitimate copyright issues.

For requests involving harassment, doxxing, privacy concerns, or safety issues (not copyright), email: ogdeig@protonmail.com with a subject line like “Urgent Safety” or “Removal Request”. Include the relevant Civic Threat URL(s) and a clear explanation.
