1) Who we are

Civic Threat is operated by Civic Threat Productions (United States). Questions: ogdeig@protonmail.com

2) What are cookies and similar technologies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. Local storage (such as localStorage) stores small amounts of data in your browser. We may also use similar technologies like pixel tags and server logs to deliver and protect the site.

3) How we use cookies and local storage

We may use these technologies for:

  • Essential operation: loading pages, basic functionality, preventing errors
  • Security: abuse prevention, rate limiting, bot mitigation, DDoS protection
  • Preferences: remembering settings like age/consent gate confirmation
  • Analytics: understanding how the site is used so we can improve it
  • Embedded content: third-party embeds may set their own cookies (see Section 6)

4) Common cookie categories

Category Purpose Examples
Strictly Necessary Required for core site functionality and security. Security cookies, load balancing, basic preferences needed to access features.
Preferences Remember your settings so you don’t have to re-enter them. Age/consent gate preference saved in localStorage; UI state.
Analytics Help us understand site usage to improve performance and content. Traffic measurement, page views, referral sources, aggregated usage patterns.
Third-Party Set by embedded services you interact with (not controlled by us). Facebook embeds or other platform embeds; video players; social widgets.

5) Age/consent gating storage

Certain sections may require you to confirm you are an adult (for example, 21+). To prevent repeated prompts, we may store a local preference (using cookies and/or localStorage). This is stored on your device and is not a government ID check.

6) Third-party embeds and their cookies

Some pages include embedded posts or content hosted by third parties (for example, Facebook). Those services may set cookies or collect data when you interact with their embedded content, according to their own privacy policies.

If you want to limit third-party tracking, consider logging out of the third-party service before visiting, or blocking third-party cookies in your browser settings.

7) How to control cookies

You can control cookies and similar technologies in several ways:

  • Browser settings: block or delete cookies; block third-party cookies
  • Private browsing/incognito: may limit persistence of cookies/local storage
  • Device settings: on some devices, you can limit ad tracking

Note: Blocking cookies may impact features such as remembering the age/consent gate, navigation state, or usage analytics that helps us improve reliability.

8) Analytics

We may use analytics tools to understand site traffic and improve performance. Analytics generally collect aggregated information such as page views, approximate location, device type, and referral source. Where required, we will request consent for analytics cookies.

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Updates will be posted on this page and the “Last updated” date will change.
